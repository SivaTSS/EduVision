Trichomonas vaginalis is a single-celled, flagellated protozoan parasite that causes trichomoniasis, a sexually transmitted infection (STI) in humans. It is the most common non-viral STI in the world. The parasite is transmitted through sexual contact and can cause symptoms such as vaginal discharge, itching, and irritation.
