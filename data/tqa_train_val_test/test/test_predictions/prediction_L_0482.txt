  Centipedes and Millipedes
    Habitat
      Centipedes: moist forests
      Millipedes: moist forests, grasslands, semi-arid habitats, deserts
    Diet
      Centipedes: nighttime predators, insects, spiders, small invertebrates, lizards
      Millipedes: herbivores, decaying plant material
    Legs
      Centipedes: one pair per segment
      Millipedes: two pairs per segment
    Classification
      Myriapoda: 13,000 species
        Chilopoda: centipedes
        Diplopoda: millipedes
        Symphyla: symphylans
        Pauropoda: pauropods
    Characteristics
      Antennae: single pair
      Eyes: simple
    Behavior
      Centipedes: fast, venomous, guard eggs and young
      Millipedes: slow, non-poisonous, protect eggs with hard soil nest
    Size
      Centipedes: from half an inch to 12 inches
      Millipedes: small, translucent, 0.5-2.0 mm long
    Distribution
      Centipedes: all continents except Antarctica
      Millipedes: all continents except
