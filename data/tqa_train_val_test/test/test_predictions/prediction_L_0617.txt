mindmap
  Nonvascular Plants
    Definition
      Lack seeds and vascular tissue
      Examples: mosses, hornworts, liverworts
    Characteristics
      Short in height
      Lack true roots, stems, and leaves
      Leafy appearance
      Stem-like and root-like structures
    Phyla
      Mosses
        Recognized as green fuzz on damp rocks and trees
        Tiny stem-like and leaf-like structures
        Gametophyte stage
        Sporophyte stage with sporangium
      Hornworts
        Named for hornlike sporophytes
        Gametophytes in flattened lobes
        Grow in moist and humid areas
      Liverworts
        Two distinct appearances: leafy or flattened
        Can resemble a liver
        Thallose liverworts: flattened, ribbon-like body
    Reproduction
      Nonvascular seedless plants
      Bryophytes: mosses, hornworts, liverworts
      Gametophyte stage
        Haploid, one set of chromosomes
        Produce gametes
      Sporophyte stage
        Diploid, two sets of chromosomes
        Forms sporangium
