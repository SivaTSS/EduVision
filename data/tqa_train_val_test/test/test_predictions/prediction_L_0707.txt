  Vertebrate Characteristics
    Definition
      Animals with a backbone
      Includes mammals, birds, reptiles, amphibians, and fish
    Vertebral Column
      Protects spinal cord
      Key feature shared by all vertebrates
    Cranium (Skull)
      Protects brain
      Head region with sense organs
    Internal Skeleton
      Supports animal
      Protects organs
      Allows movement
    Size Range
      Carp (0.3 inches) to Blue Whale (110 feet)
    Classification
      Seven classes of vertebrates
        Agnatha
          Jawless fishes
        Chondrichthyes
          Cartilaginous fishes
        Osteichthyes
          Bony fishes
        Amphibia
          Amphibians
        Reptilia
          Reptiles
        Aves
          Birds
        Mammalia
          Mammals
    Characteristics
      No jaws or scales (Agnatha)
      Skeletons of hard, rubber-like cartilage (Chondrichthyes)
      Skeletons of bone (Osteichthyes)
