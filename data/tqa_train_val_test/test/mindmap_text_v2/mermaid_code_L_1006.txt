  Replication in Science
    What is Replication?
      Definition: Getting the same result when an experiment is repeated
    Importance of Replication
      Validates Research: Ensures results are likely correct
      Acceptance of Results: Investigations need to be repeated for credibility
      Hypothesis to Theory: Repeated replication can elevate hypotheses to theories
      Incorrect Results: Non-replicable results are likely incorrect
    Case Study: Vaccine-Autism Study
      Initial Claim: Link between vaccine and autism published in 1998
      Consequences: Parents refused vaccination, leading to epidemics and deaths
      Replication Attempts: Other researchers could not replicate the findings
      Outcome: Original study deemed fraudulent; author had financial motives
      Importance of Replication: Truth revealed through replication efforts
