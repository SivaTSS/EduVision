  observation
    What is an observation?
      Definition: Information gathered with the senses
      Role: Raises questions for scientific investigations
      Role: Helps gather evidence
    Senses used for observations
      Vision
      Hearing
      Touch
      Smell
      Taste
      Instruments
        Microscopes
          Extend vision for small objects
        Telescopes
          Extend vision for distant objects
        Thermometers
          Extend touch for precise temperature measurement
    Importance of observations in scientific investigations
      Evidence gathering
        Chemical changes
          Color change
          Odor production
          Gas bubbles
        Example: Observing pennies
          New pennies: shiny and copper colored
          Old pennies: dull and brown
          Change in color indicates chemical change
      Observing temperature changes
        Sense of touch
