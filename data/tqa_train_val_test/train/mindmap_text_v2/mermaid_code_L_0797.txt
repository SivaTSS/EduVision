mindmap
  Alloys
    Definition
      Mixture of metal with other elements
      Examples: steel, bronze, brass
    Properties
      More useful than pure metals
      Can include metals, nonmetals, or both
    Formation
      Melting metal
      Dissolving other elements
      Cooling and hardening
    Common Alloys
      Brass
        Alloy of copper and zinc
        Softer and easier to shape
        Used in saxophones, doorknobs, plumbing fixtures
      Stainless Steel
        Contains nickel, chromium, carbon, iron
        Shiny, strong, rust-resistant
        Used in sinks, utensils
      Gold Jewelry
        Alloy of gold, copper, silver
        Harder than pure gold
      Bronze
        Alloy of copper and tin
        First alloy made
        Used in statues, coins
    Sterling Silver
      Contains 93% silver, 7% copper
      Harder and stronger than pure silver
      Retains malleability and luster
