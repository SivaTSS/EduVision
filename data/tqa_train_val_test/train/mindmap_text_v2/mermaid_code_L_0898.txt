  External Combustion Engines
    Definition
      Combustion Engine
        Burns fuel to produce thermal energy
        Uses thermal energy to do work
    Types
      External Combustion Engine
        Burns fuel externally
        Example: Steam Engine
      Internal Combustion Engine
    Functioning
      Heating Water
        Produces steam
        Steam under pressure
      Piston Movement
        Moves back and forth inside cylinder
        Kinetic energy of piston
        Can do work (turn wheels, turbines)
    Components
      Cylinder
      Piston
      Crankshaft
