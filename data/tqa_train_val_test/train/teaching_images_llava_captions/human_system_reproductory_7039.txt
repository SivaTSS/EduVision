The ovaries are located on either side of the uterus and are responsible for producing eggs. The fallopian tubes, also known as oviducts, connect the ovaries to the uterus and facilitate the passage of eggs. The uterus is the hollow, muscular organ where the fertilized egg implants and develops into a fetus. The cervix is the lower, narrow part of the uterus that leads to the vagina. The vagina is the muscular canal that extends from the cervix to the outside of the body and serves as the passageway for the baby during childbirth.
