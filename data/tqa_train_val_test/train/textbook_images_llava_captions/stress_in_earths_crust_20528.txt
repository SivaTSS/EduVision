The Grand Staircase, also known as the Grand Staircase-Escalante National Monument, is a large geological formation in the United States. It is located in southwestern Utah and is part of the Colorado Plateau. The Grand Staircase consists of a series of cliffs, canyons, and plateaus carved out by the Colorado River and its tributaries over millions of years.
