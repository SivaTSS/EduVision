mindmap
  Organic Compounds
    Macromolecules
      Proteins
        Composition: C, H, O, N, S
        Monomers: Amino acids
        Functions: Enzymes, muscle fibers, antibodies
        Unique Proteins: 100,000+ combinations
      Carbohydrates
        Composition: C, H, O
        Monomers: Monosaccharides
        Types: 
          - Simple sugars (e.g., glucose)
          - Polysaccharides (e.g., starch, glycogen)
        Function: Energy storage
      Lipids
        Composition: C, H, O, P
        Types: Fats, oils, waxes, steroids
        Functions: Long-term energy storage, cell membranes
      Nucleic Acids
        Composition: C, H, O, P, N
        Types: DNA, RNA, ATP
        Functions: Genetic information storage, protein synthesis
    Carbon's Importance
      Versatility: Forms stable bonds
      Building Blocks: Essential for macromolecules
    Functional Groups
      Role: Determine properties of organic compounds
      Importance: Influence reactivity and solubility
